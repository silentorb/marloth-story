# Don’t get too technical with magic


# Overview

In the age of Brandon Sanderson, it’s tempting to double down on the technical clarity of Marloth’s magic system.

Yet I’ve always shied away from fully fleshing out the magic.

The main reason is I want Marloth to be [Epic Wonderland](epic-wonderland-c06ed306eab048c48b8220fefd1913f7.md).  To walk a fine line between logic and whimsy.

Along those lines, magic looses some of its wonder when it becomes systematically dissected and laid bare.

<aside>
💡

Sanderson partially manages to maintain some wonder by relying on spectacle.  For [TWOLD](twold-e028aa0786f5449984a4f497c1d746fa.md)  I am increasingly trying to minimize spectacles and rely primarily on iconic imagery.  (I was already leaning heavily in that direction for the first book—I’m just trying to take that mix a step farther.)

</aside>

# Wizardry as a fantasy subset

However, I am wondering if I can treat wizardry as a subset of magic.

Already, I’ve been unofficially dividing the fantasy mechanics into sub-categories, maybe I could formalize that.
