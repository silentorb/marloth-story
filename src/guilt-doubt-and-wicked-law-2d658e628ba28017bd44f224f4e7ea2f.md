# Guilt, doubt, and wicked law

The term “Thou shalt not” is not only used by God in the Old Testament, it is sometimes also used by the world as a form of reverse morality.

This can be used as an attempt to shame people away from righteousness and cast doubt on what righteousness and wickedness really are.

This is usually most effective when it is a wicked **status quo.**

# Use

This mechanic can be effective in story writing to add tension, drama, and mystery.

## Conflict

This helps support [Ideological contests](../Features/Ideological%20contests%203095477179ab4fd1a6977f151ff2aef8.md).

## Mystery

Instead of having a gray world where there is no good or evil, this instead makes it less clear what the good and evil are, but the clarity is simply shrouded—there is still good and evil.
