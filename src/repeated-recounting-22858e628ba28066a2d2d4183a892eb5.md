# Repeated recounting

[Products: TWOLD](twold-e028aa0786f5449984a4f497c1d746fa.md)

# Anecdotal

I don’t think of myself as a storyteller, but I guess I am.

I love recounting events to people.

In a way, I enjoy recounting something more than hearing it for the first time from someone else.

# The problem

In general, modern authors are afraid of redundancy.  If an event happens and one character needs to recount that event to another, the recounting is skipped.

I don’t like that.  When people are reunited, for me the payoff is the recounting.

Skipping that is like buying an ice cream cone and dumping out the ice cream.

# Classic literature

The further back in time one goes, the more common it is for authors to intentionally repeat themselves.

# The Bible

The Bible in particular has many redundant accounts.

The Gospels describe Jesus ministry four time from different perspectives.

The account of the Children of Israel being freed from Egypt and brought to the promised land is recounted multiple times throughout the Bible.

# Parallel teams

Within the context of parallel action, recounting is particularly fun when separated bands of heroes are reunited and update each other about their respective adventures.
