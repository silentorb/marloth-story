# Avoid sensational villainy

Avoid depictions of villainy that make sin look fun.

Avoid depicting scintillating acts of villainy.

No Joker.

Villains can be sympathetic.  Villains can be cool.  But separate the sympathy and coolness from their deeds.

Jakob Damond was the coolest villain of the first book, and in many ways he was similar to and influenced by the Joker.  However, the audience rarely saw his villainy—it was mostly implied, and when he it was depicted, it was subtle and underplayed, such as when he tempted people.  The story never encouraged any voyeuristic fascination to watch him do horrible things.
