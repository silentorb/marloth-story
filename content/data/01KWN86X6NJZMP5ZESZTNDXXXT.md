Similar to [[01KWN86X6NJZMP5ZESZTNDXY25]], what if I create a rule-of-thumb for ambient society?

Instead of having the main characters in the midst of lots of society, create a sense of society being just around the corner or in the background.

Reflecting back on [[01KWN86X6NJZMP5ZESZTNDXY6D]], I often subconsciously employed this constraint.

# The play effect

However, I want to be careful, because I don't want to accidentally invoke the "play effect".

The play effect is when the story feels like it is a play, with the world restricted to the stage.

While many playwrights have used much ingenuity over the centuries to work within the confines of a stage, it is still an artificial restriction I've never liked.

# Social destination

[[01KWN86X6NJZMP5ZESZTNDXXXZ]], has the pattern of starting the player in a small location and revealing a spacious location in the distance, which the player gradually makes his way toward.

Maybe I can employ a similar pattern with society.

Maybe there can still be occasions with crowds of people in the foreground, but keep those situations as carrots in the distance which the main characters are working toward?
