# Magic industry

When a business applies engineering to [magic](./1c058e628ba280b0b2e6c395dd9daddb.md) to produce technology for general consumption, with the goal of being financially sustainable.

> 💡 At the time of this writing, this feature has no [TWOLD inspirations](./2eea538996934ce8abafc27132e576c1.md).
> I’ve been trying to mitigate features with no inspirations because it adds further noise when I’m trying to sift through features to associate with inspirations.
> However, now I’m thinking it might be useful to distinguish cases where I have a feature that has no media inspirations, at least not from any of my recorded [TWOLD inspirations](./2eea538996934ce8abafc27132e576c1.md).
> I’m sure I’ve seen this feature a few times, but very rarely.

# Business

For a case to fall under this feature, it must involve a formal business venture.

Making a significant profit from technology requires a team.

It isn’t practical for a lone mad scientist to make a steady profit from technology.

In the real world, solo inventors may make a few inventions that make them money, but real monetization of an invention requires sales, marketing, QA, customer service, etc.
