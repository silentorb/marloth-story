# A World of Themes

> 💡 These are Marloth notes I wrote 8/3/2003 6:32 pm.

Here are some of the main themes running through Marloth.  Stick to them,
not simply because it gives things consistency and depth, but because
I all have too many.  Not that I *really*
believe that.

- Purity
- Perversion
- Death/ zombies
- Toys/ entertainment
- Dreams / Fantasy / Fairytales
- Books
- Children & Adults / Growing up
- Madness / Denial?

Here a step by step list of rules for creating and writing new content.

# 1. Atmosphere

- Always ask yourself “What is the mood of this scene?”. 
Generally, I don’t think the tone has shifted too much in the midst
of scenes.  Each individual scene has a fairly consistent mood. 
Though there are subtle undertones that sometimes pop up yet aren’t
dominating the scene, such as a splash of sudden creepy, cute, or
humor.

# 2. Deja Vu

- Whenever possible, bring back elements and aspects from previous
parts of the story.  You can reference the list of themes above for
good ideas.

# 3. Symbolism

- Try to make the details themselves subtly reflect the struggles
that are going on in the story.

# 4. Mystery

- Don’t give any more detail than is absolutely necessary.  Don’t
feel that you have to keep explaining things, unless the explanation
is ironic or in someway inciteful.
