# Pacing Dilemma

I love fast paced stories that span short periods of time, but most of the stories I want to tell don’t fit that.  Or do they?

# Resolution

For now I’m experimenting with worrying less about pacing.

In some respects, I think my concerns with pacing are derived from more fundamental issues I should focus on instead:

1. A rich setting supporting the intended stories.
2. Only covering events that are really interesting to me.

I suspect that if I focus on those two items, much of the pacing will fall into place.

> 💡 There’s nothing wrong with a slow pace if it’s all interesting.  The problems arise when storytellers dally on tedious steps.

# Previous

> 💡 I’ve been thinking about this more and am considering just experimenting and structuring the story line after James Adelle wake up in the Asylum as a steady stream.  If it doesn’t work I may be able to just break up the pacing without drastic rewriting.
> I’m starting to form a theory that it is easier to break up a tight pacing than to convert a slow pacing into a fast pacing.
> Slow pacing is easier to write.  It’s like playing a game of tag where you can say, “Time out!” any moment you like.  Need a day to pass?  Let it pass.  Need a month to pass?  Let it pass.
