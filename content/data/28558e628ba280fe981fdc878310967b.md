# Temporal and spatial mechanics of survival horror

# Spatial

Extrinsic [[dc101b6438cf43a8b5f1e17212b8c950]] dangers roughly fall under two categories:

1. Mobile
2. Static

Mobile dangers roam around, while static dangers affect an area.

# Temporal

Mobile dangers are affected by time in that they can only be in one location at any given time, and that location can change.

Static dangers can also be affected by time, in that some can only be active at certain times.

For example, some areas may only be dangerous at night or they can be seasonal.

# Enemy awareness

Most mobile dangers are sentient.

A key mechanic of mobile spatial danger is:

1. Whether the survivor is aware of the monster
2. Whether the monster is aware of the survivor
