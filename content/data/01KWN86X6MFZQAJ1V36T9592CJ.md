# Distinguishing TWOLD haunted home from possession storylines

Some [[01KWN86X6NJZMP5ZESZTNDXXY1]] stories involve possession, which is closer to what I’m looking for.

I have no no interested in a possession storyline, but since there are some similarities, it would be good to clarify the differences:

1. Possession storylines usually eventually shift to being seen from other people’s point of view—people who are in danger from the possessed person.  (Example: The Shining).
2. Once the character is possessed, it is no longer their will which is in control of their body, and from that point they really aren’t the same character.
3. Sometimes possession involves willpower and can be fought against, but often it either can’t be or there’s not much of a fight.
    1. Sometimes possession stories have brief moments of lucidity where the possessed character breaks free, but usually such cases are contrivances with no major significance.

There are two main things I dislike about possession storylines:

1. They have a demonic quality which is unhealthy to focus on
2. They detract from the punch of the character’s own sin.  The character’s actions and consequences become less personal and owned.
