# Background

For years, I’ve been wanting to capture the essence of a forest setting in both narrative and game form, and have struggled in both media.

In particular, I’m mainly interested in a dark, inhospitable forest.  People live in or near the forest

# Experience

I’m a city boy.  I have little experience living in the country.

The first book largely took place in urban settings.  The few scenes that took place away from civilization were usually vague, underdeveloped locations.

# Dangers

Part of the coziness is having dangers outside.  Roaming monsters and malicious supernatural entities.

- Beasts
- Witches
- Fairies / Fey
- Curses and enchantments
- Magical traps
- Shades
- Spirits / wraiths
- Lovecraftian horrors
- The undead
- Sapient trees
- Bad weather
- Bogs
- Lightning
- Foggy cliffs

# Mystery

There is something mysterious about a dark forest.  The trees and overgrowth obscures and hides things.  The twisting, organic landscape makes navigation more difficult and increases the potential for people getting turned around or missing key sections of land.

A dense forest feels alive, and is alive, even when nothing is moving.
