# Fantasy economy

# Background

Fantasy mechanics break the laws of nature.

The laws of nature are completely congruent.

Once those laws are broken, incongruency is introduced.

The incongruency is like a knot, it can be shifted from one part of the rope to another but cannot be removed.

# Optimizing fantasy

However, fantasy mechanics are often sub-optimal.

Often fantasy mechanics violate more natural laws then they need to in order to achieve the desired effect.

The full cost of the mechanic rarely properly weighed.  Likewise, more optimal alternatives are rarely explored.

Doing so is not normally encouraged.

> 💡 Brandon Sanderson has started a paradigm shift in the direction of more technically responsible mechanics.

# Conservation of energy

One of the most commonly abused natural laws is [the law of conservation of energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conservation_of_energy).

# Magic always comes with a price

This feature is *not* about the trope “Magic always comes with a price.”
