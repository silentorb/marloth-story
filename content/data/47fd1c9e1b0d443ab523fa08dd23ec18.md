There's a sublime quality to [[462f2ba05cc541d3b7550bedcd5d33f8]] NPCs that the other [[4126541b1c124056b5dff756778cdba2]] games don't have.

[[462f2ba05cc541d3b7550bedcd5d33f8]] feels like the characters are randomly dropped into an open world.

They each have their own personal problems and their own approach to the problems at large.

> 💡For the scope of this concept, I'm mainly interested in the initial introduction of NPCs, not so much the entire "questline" of each NPCs.
>
> The "questlines" in the three Souls games require the player to do esoteric steps to progress.
>
> The "questlines" in [[462f2ba05cc541d3b7550bedcd5d33f8]] are especially convoluted and buggy.

# Qualities

It's difficult for me to put my finger on what makes the [[462f2ba05cc541d3b7550bedcd5d33f8]] NPC approach so distinctive, both from the other [[4126541b1c124056b5dff756778cdba2]] games and from other media in general.

## Isolated and personal

Like other Souls games, Dark Souls 3 NPCs are isolated and spread out.

You rarely encounter more than one in NPC in a group.

That makes your dealings with each of them more personal.

## Tormented or malicious

Most of the NPCs in [[462f2ba05cc541d3b7550bedcd5d33f8]] either have immediate problems they are struggling with, or are openly antagonistic and critical.

This is in contrast to the NPCs in the previous games, where most of the characters don't have immediately striking problems, and/or are more neutral.

The NPCs in all three games are tragic, but that is a separate issue since the tragedy usually comes later and often is never even revealed unless the player does certain things.

# Purgatory

In a way, [[462f2ba05cc541d3b7550bedcd5d33f8]] has a similar quality to some narratives' depiction of purgatory.
