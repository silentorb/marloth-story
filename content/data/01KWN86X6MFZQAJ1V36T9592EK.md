# Motivation for a Dark Forest

# Overview

Why do I want to have a Dark Forest setting in a story?

Note that in the term *Dark Forest*, “Forest” is the part I am most interested.

*Dark* is secondary, but still an important clarification.

> 💡 The following sections are roughly in order of priority, from highest to lowest.

# Nature

I am a city boy who is often too disconnected from nature.

There is something healthy about nature.

> 💡 The first book had practically no nature settings.  The only times the characters are in the wilderness, it is described as barren and desolate.

# Verdant

I like greenery.  Abundant, overflowing greenery.

Some nature settings, such as deserts, are boring to me.

I want lush landscapes.

# Wild

I want the forest to feel like it has a life of its own, and it cannot be tamed by man.

In that respect, I wan this forest to effectively be more like a rain forest.

Man has learned to survive in the forest, but they do not control it.

# Maze-like

I suppose I like all of my environments to be maze-like, but forests—especially dense ones—are natural mazes.

# Community

While there may be some scenes of characters wandering the forest, most of the forest narrative will revolve around villages and dwellings.

In a way, these habitats are like islands, surrounded by a sea of trees.

# Magical

Ties in with [[01KWN86X6NJZMP5ZESZTNDXY5G]].

Also haunted, [[01KWN86X6NJZMP5ZESZTNDXY8M]], and supernatural.

# Dangerous

Even when nothing dangerous is happening, a dark forest has an air of menace.

# Isolated

While the city at night can have a sense of isolation, it is not as pure as being in the middle of a forest.

# Variety

Having an forest setting breaks up the urban paces.

# Alice in Wonderland

This is a minor motivation, but[[01KWN86X6NJZMP5ZESZTNDXY6T]] featured forest locations.

While I’m not trying to mimic [[01KWN86X6NJZMP5ZESZTNDXY6T]] beat-by-beat, it is one of my primary inspirations and it seems fitting to include such a distinct yet general enough that I am not in danger of ripping off a specific-feature.
