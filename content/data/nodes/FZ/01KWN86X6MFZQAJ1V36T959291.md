# Matching causality scale

> Every action has an equal and opposite effect.

Big effects should have big causes.

Small effects should have small causes, but that is less of an issue.

A common mistake writers make is to have small actions cause big effects.

That is erroneous thinking and subtly misleading.

> That error is partially fueled by humanism—humans thinking individual humans are more powerful than they actually are.

# Catalyst

Big effects can have small catalysts, where the catalyst triggers large actions.

In such cases, larger forces are poised to move in an unavoidable direction, it is only a matter of time before they are set in motion.

In such cases, dice are continually being rolled.  The catalyst is the first successful die roll.
