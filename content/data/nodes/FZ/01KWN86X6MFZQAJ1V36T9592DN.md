# Initially shroud the Asylum job in mystery

Originally I was planning on layout out all of the cards in the first Asylum business meeting.

However, as I keep finding more and more later meetings where it would be fun to have James explain the nature of his work, I would rather mitigate how often that information is repeated.

With that goal in mind, it would be good to say as little about the nature of the Asylum job as possible, and gradually have the agency (mostly James) figure things out.

I could have the Asylum representatives claim that the knowledge is “confidential”.  In reality, they are:

1. Embarrassed about having “stolen” wizard technology
2. Embarrassed that they don’t know how to use said technology
3. Afraid that they will lose their competitive advantage if the world knew what they were working on
4. What they are doing isn’t entirely *legal*, both in general and particularly for the Asylum.

Initially hiding these details provides many story benefits.

Initially hiding the details:

1. Reduces repetition
2. Adds [[01KWN86X6PZXQP43T36924KCSG]] 
3. Minimizes a front-heavy exposition dump
4. Adds opportunities for occasional revelation story beats
5. Increases the intended tension and foreboding surrounding this enterprise and the client
6. It increases the sense of importance in the endeavor
    1. “Whatever it is, it *must* be important!”
7. Creates a tension where the agency is asked to work blind
    1. A tension that gives James something to complain and fret about
