# Custom symbolism

This feature is not about symbolism in the traditional narrative sense.

Similar to how colors can represent completely different ideas to different people depending on culture, so to, symbolism is relative.

And symbolism is malleable.

It is useful for media to rely on previously established symbolic associations within their target culture(s).

However, media can also create its own associations.

> **It's like she only wants to hook up when Ryan comes around.** **It's getting to the point where I get excited every time I see that little dude walk through the door. - Darryl Philbin, The Office**

Taking a cue from [[01KWN86X6PZXQP43T36924KCSY]], I don’t always have to be restrained by default associations.  That show regularly creates associations that normally don’t exist.  A rocking chair can be a place to sit, an adversary, a portal to another world, a trap, a key, etc.
