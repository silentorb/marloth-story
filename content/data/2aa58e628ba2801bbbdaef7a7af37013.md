# Nivana supporting her sister

[[2aa58e628ba28096a3d8ce76019ab7b6]]

# Motivation

This story arc has the following benefits:

1. It makes [[a2ee9cd0a28b4945af74fd3022fd97af]] more sympathetic
2. It adds more [[4a141498769b409797d96a49f190e96f]].
3. It fleshes out the Mediev family dynamics
    1. Similar but slightly different: It ties together the skeletal remains of previous Mediev story plans and puts meat back on those bones
4. It adds more weight to Nivana’s [[3c8bb63bea1440cba5eb8b570cecec24]] 
5. It makes Nivana’s circumstances less about Nivana.
6. It is an unusual feature for this kind of story and adds a splash of Charles Dickens-style narrative to [[e028aa0786f5449984a4f497c1d746fa]].
7. It adds drama (without being melodramatic)

# Adelle

At one point I discarded this idea because I felt Nivana’s ongoing relationship with Jewel distracted from Nivana and Adelle’s relationship.

However, now that Adelle’s role in the setting is better established and Nivana is a secondary character, I’m less concerned about them directly relating to each other.

Increasingly, they will need to be indirectly relating to each other through their larger circumstances.

In this case, [[20f58e628ba2804dae2ceb19a9717da5]] may actually provide another dimension *for* Nivana and Adelle to relate to each other.

It’s like in rom-coms when one of the main characters is a single parent and the other main character earns favor by befriending the child.

Both Nivana and Adelle are concerned for [[20f58e628ba2804dae2ceb19a9717da5]]’s well being, and as a [former Mediev maid](./e44bfe4304c44cfca11e22c29d2a08dd.md), Adelle has history with [[20f58e628ba2804dae2ceb19a9717da5]]’s care.

[[20f58e628ba2804dae2ceb19a9717da5]] might also provide relational contrast.  Nivana is used to helping [[20f58e628ba2804dae2ceb19a9717da5]] as her younger, mild-mannered sister, while post-maid Adelle is a peer that pushes back.
