For many years I have gravitated toward fully 3D games over 2D and abstract UI games.

It recently occurred to me that part of the reason is I spend so much of my life in an abstract, 2D computer world.

They say reading is good because it exercises the imagination—well, I spend all day exercising my imagination in that way.

For someone spending their days out in the 3D physical world, coming home to play an abstract, 2D game in the evening might feel novel.

For me, what I am looking for isn't simply immersion, its tangibility.

> 💡Ironically, I'm looking for a tangibility I've never felt VR games provide.
