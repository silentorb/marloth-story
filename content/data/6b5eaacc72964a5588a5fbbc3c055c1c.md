# Interior / exterior locations

While Dishonored has a lot of good qualities, one of the main reasons I have it listed as an inspiration for Marloth narratives is the way it combines interior and exterior settings.

Few games do such a good job of having locations which consist of both rich exteriors and rich interiors.

While Dishonored isn't everything I would define as [[e5cc80dc61ed4c629951cdf472b20b7a]], it still captures a key aspect of that, particularly with its night time settings.

It is cool to wander the streets, slip into the alleys, climb up onto the roof tops, hop across balconies, then hop through a window and start exploring the interior of the buildings you were skirting.

The way the character can bounce inside then outside almost feels like some games like *Guacamelee* where the players can switch between dimensions.

The hopping in and out is accentuated by the game's stealth mechanics, where sometimes switching from interior to exterior or vice-a-versa is part of how the player can escape pursuers.
