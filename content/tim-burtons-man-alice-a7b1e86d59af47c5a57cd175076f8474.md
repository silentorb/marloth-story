# Tim Burton’s man-Alice

Tim Burton protagonists tend to be passive and rarely take up arms.

The most distinctive case of a Burton protagonist taking up a weapon and engaging in battle is in his rendition of Alice in Wonderland.

The scene where Alice dons armor and grabs a sword to fight the Jabberwock falls flat on multiple levels.

For one, it is the pinnacle of woke post-feminism.  She is clearly intended to be a beacon for female empowerment, yet her solution to empowerment is to be more masculine.  Within the armor, she looks like a man, and fights in a manly manner.  Instead of championing the distinct qualities of womanhood, the woke solution is to claim that there are no distinct qualities and women are basically men.

Mixing epic and wonderland is already difficult, don’t make it even more difficult by mixing in gender confusion.

Secondly, for some inexplicable reason (though possibly due to some of the misguided feminist goals), any Gothic tones previously present in the movie are dropped during the final battle.

The Gothic glue that worked so well in American McGee’s Alice was omitted when it was most useful.

If you’re going to have a woman beat up the bad guys with style, go with Selena from Underworld or something like that.
