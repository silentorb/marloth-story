# Elemental character

> 💡 “Elemental” is not the best word for this idea, but it is the best I can think of currently.  I feel like I’ve heard a term that fits better, something about a thing that follows its nature because that is its nature.  I thought about the term “Natural character” but that doesn’t sound right.

An elemental character is somewhat abstract and surreal.

They should have little to no backstory.

> 💡 It is fine for there to be occasional references to previous events in their lives, as long as the previous events don’t say too much about the particular character.

They should have little to no explicit motivation for their actions.

Elemental characters are forces of nature.  They behave the way they do because *that is the way they are*.

They behave according to their nature and behave otherwise.

An elemental character can change their views on matters, and grow in small ways, but beyond that, they should not have character arcs with significant change.

For Marloth, elemental characters should mostly be non-human creatures.
