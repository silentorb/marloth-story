“There is a certain patient here.  It’s an unusual case, and I can’t allow the rest of my staff to handle it.”

“The fees are a little steep.”

“You won’t even tell us what the job is,” said Deadwick.

“Well, I was thinking—James is a returning patient.  Perhaps the price could be softened with some free therapy?  Leeches, electroshock, brain surgery?”

The bear turned to James.  “What do you say, James?  Is any of that something you’re looking for?”

“No, no, I’m good!  I…we…already have health coverage.”

The bear turned back to the doctor.  “Pure cash.  Sweet and simple.”

The doctor led them through a maze of hallways and stairs, before finally arriving at lone door near the bottom of the Asylum.

“I’ll take you back to your quarters.  Since you are technically a patient, you’ll need an escort anywhere.  With some wrangling I was able to convince them to give Deadwick and I temporary escort clearance.”

He flashed a badge with James and Adelle’s names on it.

“How are your accommodations?  Is the room comfortable?”

“…It’s…well padded.”

“Good, good!  Deadwick and I have lodging on the other end with the doctors.  I would normally sleep at home, but this place is a fortress.  It’s too hard to get in and out of.”
