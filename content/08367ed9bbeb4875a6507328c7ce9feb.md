# Defining and redefining the Asylum

I need both a fresh definition of the Asylum for the first book, and a partially different definition for the Asylum in the second book.

# First Asylum

- The home of science and scientists
- The extreme of James’ weaknesses
- Prison
- Isolation
- Extreme introversion
- Torment

Really, the Asylum didn’t have a consistent identity or role, and was a pastiche of common Asylum tropes.

# Second Asylum

I want the second Asylum to have a slightly more well defined identity.  It can still have multiple roles and may have a more random and cosmopolitan flavor, but I want the symbolism and meaning to be a little more consistent.

- The doctors are the judges of the patients, labeling features as faults and using any means necessary to eradicate those faults
- Intellectual oppression
- Lack of empathy

> 💡 In the first Asylum, James was the only visible patient.
> This second round should be less about James and more about other patients.

# Thoughts

- The patients are the unlovely in need of much love
    - Society needs to do something with them
    - Asylum’s are created to isolate them
    - An Asylum is a gathering place for madness
    - Asylum caregivers can be worn down and discouraged

> 🪄 Fantastic premise: The Asylum is a gathering place for madness, where the madness festers and turns into something malevolent
