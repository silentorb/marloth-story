# James looking like a transcendent robot

# Success in the first book

In the first book, I wanted James to be an odd mix of disconnected and passionate, and that worked due to the roles and context he was placed in.

# Problems in the second book

With new roles and context, I’ve had a hard time maintaining that balance in the second book.

Without even writing many scenes, I can already see how James is in danger of becoming a robot—so ethereal and detached from his immediate circumstances that he doesn’t seem to care about anything.
